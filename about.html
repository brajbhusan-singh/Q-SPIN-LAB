About Q-SPIN LAB

Advancing Spintronics, Quantum Materials and Artificial Intelligence for Future Information Technologies

About the Laboratory

The Quantum Spintronics Laboratory (Q-SPIN LAB) is a research group in the Department of Physics, School of Basic and Applied Sciences at Harcourt Butler Technical University (HBTU), Kanpur. The laboratory is dedicated to experimental and computational research in condensed matter physics, spintronics, nanomagnetism, and quantum materials.

Our research combines advanced thin-film engineering, magnetic characterization, spin transport measurements, and artificial intelligence to discover and optimize next-generation materials and devices for low-power information technologies.

Vision

To establish Q-SPIN LAB as a globally recognized center for excellence in spintronics, altermagnetism, topological materials, and quantum technologies through innovation, interdisciplinary collaboration, and high-impact scientific research.

Mission

  • Advance frontier research in Spintronics and Quantum Materials.
  • Develop energy-efficient spin-based electronic devices.
  • Integrate Artificial Intelligence with Materials Discovery.
  • Train the next generation of researchers and engineers.
  • Promote international collaborations and open scientific research.

Research Philosophy

Scientific breakthroughs emerge from the synergy of fundamental physics, advanced materials engineering, precision experimentation, computational modeling, and artificial intelligence. Q-LAB is committed to creating an environment that encourages curiosity, collaboration, and innovation.

Collaborations

The laboratory actively welcomes collaborations with universities, national laboratories, research institutes, and industrial partners working in condensed matter physics, spintronics, nanotechnology, and quantum information science.
