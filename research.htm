Research

Exploring Spin, Topology and Quantum Materials for Next-Generation Information Technologies

Research Overview

The Quantum Spintronics Laboratory performs interdisciplinary research combining condensed matter physics, nanotechnology, materials science and artificial intelligence to develop next-generation spin-based electronic devices.

Magnetic Tunnel Junctions

Design and fabrication of high-performance magnetic tunnel junctions for MRAM, spin logic and magnetic sensing technologies.

Altermagnetic Spintronics

Experimental realization and optimization of CrSb-based altermagnetic tunnel junctions with AI-assisted materials engineering.

Topological Quantum Materials

Investigation of spin transport, spin Hall effect, Rashba-Edelstein effect and interface magnetism in topological systems.

Magnetic Skyrmions

Study of topological spin textures for future neuromorphic computing and ultra-low-power memory technologies.

Spin Pumping

Generation and manipulation of pure spin currents through ferromagnetic resonance and spin-orbit coupling phenomena.

AI for Materials Discovery

Integration of machine learning with experimental physics for intelligent optimization of spintronic materials and devices.

Experimental Expertise

  • Thin Film Growth
  • Magnetron Sputtering
  • Ion Beam Sputtering
  • Ferromagnetic Resonance
  • Spin Pumping Measurements
  • Magnetotransport Characterization
  • Magneto-Optic Kerr Effect
  • SQUID Magnetometry
  • Device Fabrication

Research Vision

Q-LAB aims to become a globally recognized research center in Spintronics, Quantum Materials, Altermagnetism and Artificial Intelligence driven Materials Science by fostering interdisciplinary collaborations and high-impact scientific discoveries.
